Alignment

Company Name

Alignment Nashville


How Used

Alignment is the principle of design that correlates to how the left, right, centered, or left-and-right justification of text can help organize information. Generally, text should be left-justified in left-to-right reading languages, and right-justified in right-to-left reading languages. In the caption from Alignment Nashville, it is easy to visibly see the logo and menu are centered, while the text is left-justified (even within the photo). This makes reading easier and boost comprehension. Generally, text should not be centered unless it only contains a small amount of information. When pictures are used, pictures should generally be placed to the right of left-justified text, and vice-versa. This can be easily seen on the pages of this Design Principles site, with the text left-justified on the left of the screen, and the screenshots on the right of the screen. When only small amounts of information can be displayed at a time, it may be appropriate to center the text and photos. By following the rules of alignment, webpages can be read more easily, and pictures can be appreciated more fully.
